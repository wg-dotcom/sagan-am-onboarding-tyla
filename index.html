02

Your role

From Account Manager to Member's Strategist

You start as an Account Manager. Over the next twelve months you progressively transition into an Account Partner, what we also call the Member's Strategist. The shift is gradual. Day one you're running operations and learning the engine. By month twelve, you're shaping how the member grows.

The job, in one line: you own the long-term relationship with a portfolio of Sagan Passport members. What "own" means deepens as you grow into the role.

The members pay us monthly. They expect a partner, not a vendor. Your job is to make sure that's what they get. Concretely, that means:

  • Running proactive alignment calls (typically monthly, more often during a pilot period or a hiring sprint)
  • Translating each member's hiring needs to the recruiter team and staying in the room until placement
  • Coordinating the operational mechanics: GPD payroll onboarding, background checks, offer letter workflow, contracts, replacements
  • Managing the active pipeline of open roles across your portfolio with a tracker the member can actually rely on
  • Handling the awkward stuff: payment chases, process changes, employee separations, escalations
  • Spotting opportunities to deepen the relationship: AI agent builds, referral partnerships, additional service lines, new departments
  • Reading the member. Knowing when they're scared, distracted, or no longer sure they need a role. Making the next move easier for them.
  • Over time, becoming the strategist they call before they make a hiring or operational decision, not after.

You will get more comfortable with all of this over the first 90 days. Nobody walks in fluent.

03

The mindset

Three things that save twelve months of trial and error

We're not a recruiting agency. We're a leverage company. The product is people plus AI agents, but the actual sell is time, focus, and operational compounding for the member.

01
The hire isn't done when the offer is signed.
That's where the real work starts. Most of our replacements happen in the 30-to-90-day window after placement. Stay close.
02
You build trust slowly and lose it fast.
Every operational miss compounds. If something inside Sagan is broken and it touches your member, it's yours to fix, regardless of which team owns it. This is non-negotiable.
03
The member is buying the relationship, not the transaction.
A churned member who liked you sends a referral. A churned member who liked the placement but not you doesn't. Always invest in the relationship.

08

The 12-month gameplan

From Account Manager to Account Partner
The path

To get there, you need to become a shark.

Tyla, I'm not hiring you to be an Account Manager for the next three years. I'm hiring you to become an Account Partner inside twelve months, your members' strategist. AP is the closer role. AP owns commercial outcomes end to end: closing new logos, expanding existing accounts, driving partner referrals, and growing the revenue line for their book of business.

A shark in this business is the person who
  • Smells the upsell before the member articulates it
  • Closes hard but never feels rushed
  • Builds a real network instead of working a CRM
  • Knows pricing cold and never apologizes for it
  • Is friendly AND direct in the same breath
  • Walks into every renewal already knowing the answer
Q1 · Months 1–3
Learn the engine
Own the AM role. Run alignment calls. Hit retention targets on your portfolio. Memorize the product stack and pricing. Sit on as many of my sales calls as your calendar allows. By end of Q1 you should be able to recite pricing, membership benefits, and referral terms in your sleep.
Success looks like 8–12 accounts owned. Zero unforced churn. One expansion conversation opened.
Q2 · Months 4–6
Develop sales instinct
Continue running your AM portfolio. Start joining 2–3 outbound sales conversations per month as a co-pilot. Take notes, listen, debrief with me after. Pick one of our existing referral partners (Tanner at iM4, Kurtis at Bison CFO, others) and learn how that partnership was built from start to first-referral.
Success looks like 1 closed expansion deal you drove. 1 referral partnership conversation opened by you. Portfolio retention stays clean.
Q3 · Months 7–9
Hybrid mode
Continue running your AM portfolio. Start owning your first outbound sales conversations end-to-end. Bring me in only when you need cover. Drive 2 net-new logos through full sales cycle (with my support). Start handling pricing negotiation yourself.
Success looks like 2 new member contracts signed (you as primary). 1 referral partner relationship deepened. Your AM book still solid.
Q4 · Months 10–12
The transition
You stop calling yourself an AM. You start running like an AP. You own a portfolio + close new logos + manage 2–3 partner relationships. I shift from your supervisor to your peer. You're closing alongside me, not under me.
Success looks like 5+ new logos closed in Q4 alone. 95%+ book retention. Indispensable to at least one partner relationship. Title changes formally. Compensation changes formally.
